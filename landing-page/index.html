Your music has no home for the stuff around the music.

Your DAW handles the sound. But the lyrics? That's in Notes. Your release schedule? Probably a calendar event you'll forget. Demo files? Downloads folder, somewhere. Session notes and ideas? Lost between three apps and a napkin.

You've got 7 songs at different stages of completion and nobody — not even you — can answer the basic questions: What still needs drums? Which vocal tracks are comped? When is the actual release date?

Not because you're disorganized. Because nothing was built to organize this stuff. Until now.

I built Forge Village because nothing else existed for the stuff around the music. There are a million tools for making music. None of them help you actually keep track of it.

Be honest with yourself

Who this is for.

It's not for everyone. Here's how to tell if it's for you.

This is for you if...

  • You have 4-12 songs at various stages of completion
  • You keep losing track of what still needs to be recorded
  • Your lyrics, demos, and notes live in different places
  • You've missed a self-imposed release deadline
  • You want one place to look at instead of five
  • You care about your data staying on your machine

This is NOT for you if...

  • You want a DAW or music production tool
  • You only work on one song at a time
  • You're looking for cloud-based collaboration
  • You love project management software and spreadsheets
  • You want AI to write your lyrics for you

What It Does (and Doesn't)

Forge Village is an organizer — it doesn't produce or edit audio. It plays back audio files you upload so you can add timestamped markers, but it's not a DAW. Think of it as the command center around your music, not the instrument.

WAV, MP3, M4A, FLAC, AIFF, and OGG. Upload any of these as assets and they'll play back with timestamped markers for notes.

Technical

Node.js + Express for the backend. Plain HTML/CSS/JS for the frontend — no React, no Vue, no build step. WaveSurfer.js for waveform visualization. It's intentionally simple so anyone can read and modify the code.

Yes — it's all plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in a single file. If you know any web dev, you can customize it. No TypeScript compiler, no bundler, no dependencies to install for the frontend.
